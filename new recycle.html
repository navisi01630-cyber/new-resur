Introduction to Recycling

Understanding the importance of recycling in Bangladesh

Why Recycling Matters

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into reusable objects to prevent waste of potentially useful materials. In Bangladesh, with rapid urbanization and population growth, recycling has become critical due to limited landfill space and environmental concerns.

Key Benefits of Recycling

  • Environmental Protection: Reduces pollution and conserves natural resources
  • Energy Conservation: Uses less energy than producing new products from raw materials
  • Economic Value: Creates jobs and generates revenue from waste materials
  • Public Health: Reduces disease vectors and contamination risks
  • Sustainability: Preserves resources for future generations

With over 25,000 tonnes of solid waste generated daily in Bangladesh, recycling conserves resources, reduces pollution, and creates economic opportunities. Scaling recycling could supply 75% of factory plastic needs, earning Tk 7 billion ($67M) annually in foreign exchange.

Impact of Recycling on Society

How recycling transforms communities and the environment

Positive Impacts

  • Poverty Reduction: Waste pickers increase earnings 25-50% with proper equipment
  • Health Improvements: Proper waste management reduces disease risks
  • Women's Empowerment: 70% of textile sorters are women with fair wages
  • Job Creation: Recycling employs 5,400+ in plastics alone
  • Resource Conservation: Reduces need for raw material extraction

Challenges to Address

  • Informal Sector: Many workers lack safety equipment and fair wages
  • Health Hazards: Exposure to toxins and unsafe working conditions
  • Infrastructure Gaps: Limited recycling facilities and technology
  • Policy Enforcement: Weak implementation of environmental regulations
  • Awareness: Limited public knowledge about proper recycling practices

Creative Recycling Innovations

Transforming waste into valuable products

Plastic Roads

Mixing plastic waste with asphalt to create durable, cost-effective roads. Each km uses 1.5 tonnes of plastic waste.

Recycled Textiles

Transforming textile waste into new fabrics and clothing items. Bangladesh recycles 28% of its textile waste.

Eco-Bricks

Plastic bottles filled with non-recyclable plastic used as building materials for low-cost housing.

Bangladesh Recycling Report

Current status, challenges, and opportunities

Current Challenges

  • Waste Volume: Urban areas generate 25,000–30,000 tonnes daily
  • Collection Gaps: Only 45–50% of urban waste formally collected
  • Landfill Overload: Dhaka's landfills exhausted, contaminating water
  • Textile Waste: Apparel sector generates 577,000 tonnes/year
  • Informal Labor: ~100,000 waste pickers without safety gear

Progress & Initiatives

  • Policy Advances: National 3R Strategy, Solid Waste Management Rules 2021
  • Community Projects: Waste Concern composting diverts 130 tonnes/day
  • Training Programs: Cordaid trained 500+ waste workers
  • Entrepreneurship: Support for 65,000+ microenterprises

Future Prospects

  • Scaling could supply 75% of factory plastic needs
  • Potential to earn Tk 7 billion ($67M) annually
  • New EU regulations creating export opportunities
  • Investment in chemical recycling and pyrolysis tech
